The Fame Era
The Fame was Lady Gaga's debut album, and the start of what would be dubbed Lady Gaga's 'Eras'. This album focused heavily on the glamorous aspects of being famous, and how anyone could be or feel famous. The music on this album was heavily influenced by 80's synth dance music and ballads. The singles from this album include "Just Dance", "Beautiful, Dirty, Rich", "Poker Face", "Eh, Eh (Nothing Else I Can Say), "LoveGame", and "Paparazzi". The Fame Monster
Due to the success of The Fame, Lady Gaga released The Fame Monster EP to build on the themes she had explored on The Fame. However, instead of focusing on the glamorous side of fame, she focused on the dark side of fame and the fears she has about her newfound fame. Each song explored a fear she had. Bad Romance expresses her fear of love, Alejandro expresses her fear of men, Speechless expresses her fear of death, Dance in the Dark expresses her fear of what fame does to people, Telephone expresses her fear of commitment, So Happy I Could Die expresses her fear of being addiction, and Teeth explores her fear of the truth. A notable moment from this era is her iconic meat dress, which she said she wore because "the paparazzi was already treating [her] like a piece of meat".
Born This Way
Lady Gaga began work on her third studio album while she was touring The Fame Monster Ball. People who worked close with her said she would constantly write and record songs on the road, most of which didn't end up on her 16 track album. Her title track, Born This Way, was a self-love song written for the LGBTQ+ community that she wrote in 10 minutes. Her other single, The Edge of Glory was written as in memory of her grandfather who she claimed was her biggest fan. Joanne
Lady Gaga's performance of Paparazzi during the 2009 MTV Video Music Awards was a cultural reset if I had ever seen one. After being flamed by tabloids all year for her costumes and antics, Lady Gaga took the stage at the VMA's to show the effects that tabloid media had on artists, like Britney Spears and Lindsay Lohan. Her performance had her pulling all the stunts, like performing a random piano solo and ending the performance with her hanging herself on stage covered in blood. Even the celebrities that scoffed at her when she was announced gave her a standing ovation.
